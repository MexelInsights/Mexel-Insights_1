Example Constraint Chain — AI Infrastructure & Grid
Headline
AI data-centre build-out is accelerating
Obvious explanation
Power demand is rising — utilities need more capacity
↓ Hidden constraint
Large power transformers / grain-oriented electrical steel / high-voltage switchgear — lead times have extended to 24–48 months
Process reason
LPT manufacturing is a global oligopoly; GOES mills book 12–18 months forward; qualified EPC crews are fully booked. None of these can be resolved in under 2–3 years.
Transmission chain
AI load growth → Grid interconnection requests surge → Transformer order backlog >36 months → Utility interconnection queue extends → Data-centre commissioning delayed 1–3 years
Trigger to watch
Transformer lead times rising above 36 months in OEM earnings commentary
What would prove this wrong
New Western LPT factories announced and funded; AI demand profile shifts to nuclear or storage

FEATURED CONSTRAINT CHAINS

Three chains in detail.

Semiconductor
The Fluorine Chain
China controls 55–60% of acid-grade fluorspar. Every fluorinated semiconductor input — HF, WF6, NF3, LiPF6, UF6 — traces back to acidspar. A single export-quota tightening hits fabs, batteries, and nuclear simultaneously.
China fluorspar controls→ HF supply pressure→ WF6 / NF3 / LiPF6 constraints→ Fab throughput + battery electrolyte margins

Grid Equipment
AI Load → Grid Bottleneck
AI data-centre load growth is driving transformer orders at rates not seen since 2002. LPT lead times have quietly extended to 24–48 months. The commissioning delay is a physical constraint that cannot be resolved with capital alone.
AI load growth→ Interconnection queue→ Transformer / GOES backlog→ Data-centre commissioning delayed 1–3 years

Industrial Mfg
Gas Turbine Duopoly
GE Vernova and Siemens Energy control large-frame gas turbine supply. Order books extend past 2027–2028. The constraint is not design or permitting — it is single-crystal blade casting capacity at Howmet and Precision Castparts.
Firm capacity demand→ OEM order saturation→ Blade casting bottleneck→ Power capacity unavailable until 2028–2030
